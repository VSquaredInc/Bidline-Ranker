How to Use This Tool

Overview

This tool reads your Atlas Air bid documents and ranks every eligible bidline based on your personal preferences — desired days off, pay guarantee, and destination airports. The highest-ranked line is the best match for what you've told it matters most.

Step 1 — The Three PDFs

  • Bidline Schedule — The published bidline PDF from AIMS (e.g., April 2026 Bidlines ORD 747 FO.pdf). Contains the full schedule grid: days off (X markers), trip codes, departure airports, and deadhead legs for every line. Make sure you use the file for your crew position — CA and FO bidlines are separate documents.
  • Line Credit – Month 1 — The single-month credit value report (e.g., 747 Lines Apr26–Apr26 Period.pdf). Contains guarantee hours, TAFB, and days off per line for the first month.
  • Line Credit – Month 2 — The two-month cumulative credit value report (e.g., 747 Lines Apr26–May26 Period.pdf). Required to correctly average guarantee values for Bid Period 2 lines. If you are bidding a single bid period, upload the same single-month report here.

Step 2 — Preferences

  • Dates Desired Off — Enter the dates you want off. Only lines where every requested date is an off day (or falls after the line's schedule end) will appear in results. You can add multiple ranges.
  • Time Multiplier — Controls how much weight days off carry in the ranking score relative to guarantee hours. At 1.0, each day off is valued at its CRT rate of 4.85 credit hours (no overtime assumed). Raise it to reflect overtime expectations: 1.41 approximates picking up one overtime day per bid period (~6.85 hrs/day effective), 1.82 approximates two overtime days (~8.85 hrs/day). Lower it below 1.0 to rank guarantee pay higher than days off. Set it to 0 to rank lines purely by average guaranteed credit hours, ignoring days off entirely.
  • Line Type Filter — Primary lines are the lines you can hold based on seniority; Secondary lines are fallback holds; Reserve lines are on-call. Line subtypes are Int (International), AdH (Ad-Hoc), and 60-day variants of each. Filter to Primary Only for the most relevant results, or use Primary & Secondary to exclude reserve.
  • Line Duration Filter — Bid Period 1 lines cover a single bid period; Bid Period 2 lines span two consecutive bid periods. Filter if you want to compare only one type.
  • Airport Preferences — Enter airports you prefer or want to avoid (comma-separated IATA codes). A non-absolute preference applies a percentage bonus or penalty to Effective Value. Checking Absolute completely excludes lines that don't meet the criteria.

Understanding the Results

  • Eff. Value — The composite score used for ranking. Higher is better.
  • Avg Guarantee / Avg Days Off — Both are averaged to represent a single bid period, so Bid Period 1 and Bid Period 2 lines are directly comparable.
  • Front DH — Number of trips where you deadhead commercially from base to start the pairing.
  • Back DH — Number of trips where you deadhead commercially to base to end the pairing.
  • Trips — Date ranges of each trip pairing in the bid period.
  • Airports — All airports visited during the bid period.

Scoring Formula

Effective Value = Avg Guarantee + (Avg Days Off × 4.85 × Time Multiplier) Airport whitelist bonus: Effective Value × (1 + Bonus% / 100) Airport blacklist penalty: Effective Value × (1 − Penalty% / 100)

Both Avg Guarantee and Avg Days Off are averaged across bid periods so Bid Period 1 and Bid Period 2 lines are directly comparable.

Example: a line with 75 hrs guarantee, 12 days off, and Time Multiplier 1.2 scores:
75 + (12 × 4.85 × 1.2) = 75 + 69.84 = 144.84

Time Multiplier guidance: 1.0 = pure CRT value (4.85 hrs/day); 1.41 ≈ one overtime day per period; 1.82 ≈ two overtime days per period.
